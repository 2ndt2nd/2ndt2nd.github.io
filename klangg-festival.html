Introduction
This project focuses on art direction and user experience for Nouvel Opera Fibourg's music opera, Klanggg Festival. The five-week project involves client research, design studies, and visual expermentation with the end goal of creating an interactive landing page with strong visual direction. During the project, I took the initiative as the team's art direction lead and developed strong visual identity across unique designs.

Designer inspiration study

DAN FRIEDMAN

Dan Friedman is a notable figure in graphic design who followed Wolfgang Weingart and Armin Hoffman. He was a figure who influenced post-modern design and new wave typography.

We used Friedman's works as a starting point for our art direction, focusing on his methods of breaking conventional Swiss Design and exploring unique alternatives.

Opposing Tension

The use of elements at opposing ends of the composition creates strong figure-ground relation. The visuals come together as a cohesive whole and creates a larger combined form with strong visual draw.

layered Collage

The overlapping of elements leads to a distinct hierarchy between multiple points of interest. When executed properly, the composition becomes rich and layered with pronounced spatial depth.

textural contrasts

Utilizing dithered textures  created unique figure-ground relations and adding more draw to the textured visuals. Textures can also imitate transparency, contributing to hierarchies.

Event posters

Precedent works are used to create posters and find promising visual directions. Through creating iterative variations, we presented the best three to the teaching team for critique.

In order to expand further, our art directions need to be anchored more tangibly as principles to recreate more successful designs. With this established, our team can continue to work with a solid foundation.

Design precedents

As a learning designer, it's important to select professional quality precedents to guide a great project. Elements such as "layered elements" allow a better understanding of experimental design.

iterating designs

It is important that design is not just thought out, but laid out and put into practice. I create numerous iterations and variety for my designs to test out different ideas to find the best outcome.

MICROSITE DEVELOPMENT

collage style navigation

The collage present in the landing page provides easy navigation to access the opera shows' pages. With a hover interaction, one of the four options are highlighted and provided with a brief description. Clicking on the option then opens the page for the individual opera shows.

collage design

I used collage as a theme which represents "parts that make a whole". Using earlier studies of layering and visual tension, this creates strong hierarchies of different parts while maintaining the greater unison.

visual effects

Being familiar with digital image editing, I was heavily involved in the know-how of creating certain looks and styles. This visual texturing and overlaying became a consistently used effect throughout the project.

secondary navigation

The provided menu component at the bottom could be hovered on to reveal a quick access menu as an alternative to the main navigation. This secondary navigation would be expanded to include pages such as the venue information, ticket buying and other relevant pages.

curated content

Each individual opera show's pages contains tailored content to compliment the opera experience. Adding context provides a helpful introduction to the context, which both garner interest for the current Klanggg Festival and upcoming operas in the future.
